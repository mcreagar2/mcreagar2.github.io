About Me

I am a second year graduate student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln working toward a Ph.D. in Mathematics, Ph.D minor in Statistics. I am advised by Dr. Richard Rebarber (Mathematics) and Dr. Brigitte Tenhumberg (Biological Sciences). Currently, I am interested in mathematical biology and ecological modeling. Specifically, I am working towards constructing dynamic energy budget models for plant individuals and using computational methods to investigate emergent properties at a population-level.
I am also very passionate about promoting diversity and gender equality in the mathematical sciences. As a junior at the University of San Francisco, three other female math majors and I started USF's chapter of the Association for Women in Mathematics.
I'm always looking to learn new things and meet new people, so please feel free to connect with me!
